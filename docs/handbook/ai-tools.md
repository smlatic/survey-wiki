# Chapter 12: AI Tools - Your Virtual Assistant at Sea

## Introduction: Ask a Robot (and Hope It Doesn't Become Self-Aware)

Artificial Intelligence has officially docked in the offshore world. Tools like ChatGPT can draft reports, write scripts for data processing, or even suggest troubleshooting steps for common sensor issues. Think of it as a digital assistant that helps speed up tasks, especially repetitive ones, so you can focus on higher-level decisions and hands-on work.

As a trainee, tinkering with AI platforms can sharpen your skills. Just remember, these tools aren't perfect; treat their output as a first draft, not gospel. When big decisions (like interpreting complex survey data or making project-altering recommendations) come into play, human judgment still calls the shots.

> "The most powerful technologies are the ones that empower others."

This rings especially true for AI. Instead of replacing you, tools like these empower you to do more: they free you from repetitive grunt work so you can spend time on tasks that truly impact your project, like analyzing complex bathymetry or collaborating with senior surveyors on data interpretation.

### A Note on Domain Knowledge

Technology is just a tool to amplify what you already know. Honing your geodesy, subsea engineering, or acoustic positioning know-how matters more than perfecting your programming chops. AI can handle some coding; it can't replace your specialized insight into the marine environment.

## Writing Scripts and Code Snippets: Automating Routine Tasks

One of the best ways to leverage AI is by getting it to help you create or refine scripts for automating routine tasks. Maybe you want to rename a batch of side-scan sonar files by date, or extract specific time segments from a massive ROV video dataset. What seems like a small task can snowball if you have to repeat it a hundred times.

**Script examples:**

- A file-renaming script in Python, which reads your existing filenames and appends a date stamp
- A data parser for survey logs that organizes depth readings into neat CSV files
- A batch converter that takes raw sonar data and preps it for your favorite processing software

Your supervisor or data processor might point out the best tasks to automate. As a trainee, this is your chance to learn basic coding while also spotting inefficiencies in the workflow. You're not deciding which systems get fully automated (management and senior engineers do that) but your suggestions might spark valuable improvements.

## Brainstorming, Problem-Solving & Research Support

Sometimes you just need a quick refresher on how a particular positioning system works or a concise explanation of industry jargon. AI assistants are great for summarizing these concepts in simpler terms. That said, not everything AI says is 100% correct. Use it as a stepping stone and verify crucial details through official manuals, onboard training materials, and guidance from seasoned colleagues.

### Tips on Prompting an AI

- **Be Specific:** Instead of asking "How do I calibrate USBL?" you might say, "Explain the standard box-in calibration process for Kongsberg HiPAP USBL systems in offshore surveys."
- **Provide Context:** The more background you give the AI, the more relevant the response will be.
- **Double-Check Facts:** If the result conflicts with what your project manual says, trust the manual or ask a senior team member to clarify.

## Best Practices: Verification, Ethics, and Creativity

AI is powerful, but it's still not infallible. Always verify critical information, especially if a wrong answer could derail a survey line or lead to faulty interpretations of data. Ask yourself: How serious is it if the AI is wrong? The higher the stakes, the more human oversight is needed.

1. **Verification is Key:** Review important outputs with a teammate or reference your project documentation. If you're writing a script that could overwrite files, test it on a small subset first.
2. **Mind the Ethics:** Don't feed confidential project info into public AI systems. Stick to sanitized or generic descriptions of the problem.
3. **Stay Creative:** AI can jumpstart ideas, but humans excel at creativity and nuanced understanding. Use it to spark new scripts or report templates, then refine them with your professional insight.
4. **Know When to Ask for Help:** If your script keeps failing or the AI's suggestions feel off, consult your senior surveyor or Party Chief. A two-minute chat can save hours of head-scratching.

Ultimately, AI tools let you work faster and smarter, but they can't replace your on-the-ground knowledge of offshore surveying. By combining AI-driven efficiency with your growing domain expertise, you'll become a vital resource, someone who not only masters the technology but also truly understands what the data means and how to act on it.
