# Chapter 12: AI Tools for Surveyors

## Introduction

AI has arrived in offshore surveying. Tools like ChatGPT can draft reports, write processing scripts, or suggest troubleshooting steps for common sensor issues. It's a digital assistant that speeds up repetitive tasks so you can focus on higher-level decisions and hands-on work.

As a trainee, experimenting with AI platforms will sharpen your skills. Just treat the output as a first draft, not gospel. When it comes to interpreting complex survey data or making project-altering recommendations, human judgment still calls the shots.

### A Note on Domain Knowledge

Technology is just a tool to amplify what you already know. Honing your geodesy, subsea engineering, or acoustic positioning knowledge matters more than perfecting your programming skills. AI can handle some coding -- it can't replace your specialized insight into the marine environment.

## Writing Scripts and Code Snippets

One of the best uses for AI is getting it to help you create or refine scripts for automating routine tasks. Renaming a batch of side-scan sonar files by date, extracting specific time segments from a massive ROV video dataset -- these are small tasks that snowball fast when you're doing them a hundred times.

**Script examples:**

- A file-renaming script in Python that reads your existing filenames and appends a date stamp
- A data parser for survey logs that organizes depth readings into neat CSV files
- A batch converter that takes raw sonar data and preps it for your favorite processing software

Your supervisor or data processor might point out the best candidates for automation. As a trainee, this is your chance to learn basic coding while spotting inefficiencies in the workflow. You're not deciding which systems get fully automated -- management and senior engineers own that call -- but your suggestions might spark real improvements.

## Brainstorming, Problem-Solving & Research Support

Sometimes you just need a quick refresher on how a particular positioning system works or a concise explanation of industry jargon. AI assistants are good for summarizing concepts in plain language. That said, not everything AI produces is accurate. Use it as a starting point and verify crucial details through official manuals, onboard training materials, and experienced colleagues.

### Tips on Prompting an AI

- **Be Specific:** Instead of asking "How do I calibrate USBL?" try "Explain the standard box-in calibration process for Kongsberg HiPAP USBL systems in offshore surveys."
- **Provide Context:** The more background you give the AI, the more relevant the response.
- **Double-Check Facts:** If the result conflicts with your project manual, trust the manual. Or ask a senior team member to clarify.

## Best Practices: Verification, Ethics, and Creativity

AI is powerful but not infallible. Always verify critical information, especially when a wrong answer could derail a survey line or lead to faulty data interpretation. Ask yourself: how serious is it if the AI is wrong? The higher the stakes, the more human oversight is needed.

1. **Verification is Key:** Review important outputs with a teammate or against your project documentation. If you're writing a script that could overwrite files, test it on a small subset first.
2. **Mind the Ethics:** Don't feed confidential project info into public AI systems. Stick to sanitized or generic descriptions of the problem.
3. **Stay Creative:** AI can jumpstart ideas, but humans excel at creativity and nuanced understanding. Use it to spark new scripts or report templates, then refine them with your professional insight.
4. **Know When to Ask for Help:** If your script keeps failing or the AI's suggestions feel off, talk to your senior surveyor or Party Chief. A two-minute chat can save hours of head-scratching.

AI tools let you work faster and smarter, but they don't replace your knowledge of offshore surveying. Combine AI-driven efficiency with your growing domain expertise, and you become someone who not only uses the technology but actually understands what the data means and how to act on it.
