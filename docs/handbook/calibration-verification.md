# Chapter 9: Calibration and Verification

## Why Calibration Matters

Calibration is the process of fine-tuning your equipment so the data it produces is accurate and trustworthy. If your instruments are even slightly off, your data is off -- and inaccurate data leads to costly mistakes. Simple as that.

### Documents, Guidelines, and Procedure Manuals

Most companies follow internal or industry-recognized guidelines for calibration. In practice, you'll work with:

- **Project Procedure Documents:** Custom documents prepared for a specific job, containing step-by-step instructions and acceptance criteria. If a colleague hands you a calibration checklist, keep it close.
- **Manufacturer Manuals:** GNSS receivers, USBL systems, and MBES units all come with recommended calibration intervals and methods. The manufacturer's guidelines ensure you're aligning the system as designed.
- **Industry Standards:** Organizations like IMCA provide general best practices for marine surveying. These tend to be high-level, but they're a solid baseline when your project documents aren't clear.

Read these documents thoroughly. They'll detail procedures like box-in tests for USBL or patch tests for MBES, and they specify tolerance levels for accuracy.

### Real-World Trainee Involvement

As a trainee, you might help record readings during a static GNSS test or set up reflectors for an MBES patch test. Senior surveyors or the Party Chief typically decide whether the system meets project specs or if more checks are needed, often after consulting with data processors and equipment specialists.

!!! note "It Takes Time"
    It takes experience to spot when something is off. Early in your career, lean on procedure documents and ask "Does this value look right?" Over time, you'll develop an instinct for what normal ranges look like. A USBL check that's 2 meters off is a red flag. A few centimeters might be within tolerance.

Calibration is a great skill-builder. Every time you go through the process, you deepen your understanding of the equipment, the physics behind the measurements, and the standards that govern good data.

## Verification: Confirming Your Calibration Holds Up

Calibration is about getting things right. Verification is about confirming they stay right under real-world conditions. Both are part of maintaining data integrity.

You won't be the one deciding when or how these tests occur at first -- that's the project lead's call, based on client requirements and industry standards. But witnessing these tests teaches you the process and shows you why proper verification matters.

## Documentation and Reporting

After calibrations and verification tests, results get documented. Reports detail system checks, observed biases, and corrective actions taken. Pay attention to how these reports are structured -- clear documentation makes life easier for everyone, including future surveyors who might revisit the data years later.

You might start by proofreading or adding basic info to a report. Over time, you'll learn to summarize findings and suggest follow-up actions. Major decisions (whether to re-calibrate or swap equipment) remain a team and management call, but your growing understanding will help you contribute meaningfully.

## Troubleshooting: When Things Go Wrong

In offshore surveying, things go wrong. Count on it. A sensor starts spitting erratic readings, or your MBES data suddenly looks like noise. Troubleshooting is where experience, logic, and teamwork converge. You might be asked to check cables, reboot systems, or compare notes with a data processor to pinpoint the root cause.

As a trainee, you won't be deciding to abandon a survey line or switch to backup equipment just yet. But pay close attention during troubleshooting sessions. You'll see how problems get identified, communicated, and systematically worked through -- often under the pressure of ticking clocks and mounting costs.

These real-time problem-solving experiences build confidence. They also develop skills that aren't written in any manual -- skills you'll carry throughout your career.
