# :material-compass-rose: Beginners Guide to Offshore Surveying

## Welcome Aboard

This guide is an introduction to offshore surveying for people entering the field. It covers what the job involves, what the tools do, how teams work together, and what day-to-day life looks like on a survey vessel.

Offshore surveying sits at the intersection of technology, engineering, and the ocean. The work is varied, the learning curve is real, and no two projects are exactly alike. This guide gives you a foundation to build on.

## How to Use This Guide

Each chapter covers a core topic -- positioning, sensors, team roles, safety, data, and more. Some sections get technical, but the goal is always to orient you, not overwhelm you. When you want the full detail, follow the wiki links.

You can read straight through or jump to whatever's relevant. Need to brush up on positioning before tomorrow's survey? Go to that chapter. Want to understand what the ROV team does? Skip ahead.

!!! info "How This Guide Connects to the Wiki"
    Throughout the chapters, you'll see **"Go Deeper"** boxes. These link to the wiki's
    detailed technical articles where you can read full procedures, calibration guides,
    and reference material. This guide is the starting point; the wiki has the detail.
